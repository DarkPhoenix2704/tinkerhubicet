The world is changing faster now than during any period of time in human history. The job market is rapidly changing and the tools for learning are also evolving. To survive these massive waves of change, we just have to adapt. In the process, we also need to ensure that no one gets left behind as the world goes digital. And so, we put the club in place to make sure that our young generation gets access to tools required to participate in and contribute to 21st century technology advancements. For the last five years, we have been thinking about what will help us to achieve the goal. And here’s what we found: To be flag bearers of a change of this scale, we need our students to innovate, learn to learn, solve problems and collaborate on a massive scale. We have built programs, projects and resources that revolve around these core areas.

Campus Community is a part of TinkerHub initiatives to empower students with access to the knowledge they need to thrive in the new world. Campus Community is run by students and industry experts to achieve the same goal. Together, we will help the next generation set the course for a better future. Campus Community is for school and college students who want to learn and teach technology. (We want them to understand and embrace the power of critical thinking, problem-solving, innovation and entrepreneurship. )

Creativity: In the age of information overload, getting original ideas from people has become quite a difficult task. Kids, especially, have a creative streak that they tend to shed in school owing to judgement, peer pressure and parental pressure. We believe that creativity is what sets us all apart as unique individuals and that it is indispensable to our functioning as a human and a coder. Perhaps this is what led us to zero in on creativity as a major focus area. Problem solving: Coders are fundamentally people who make life simpler by solving problems. Any budding coder will need to learn how much difference they can make with the right approach to solving a problem. Learn to learn: It is well understood that the world that we live in is under constant evolution. Various technologies present themselves at every turn of the industry. To move on ahead, you have to learn to learn new things quickly and somewhat deeply. We focus on how to get students to think for themselves and take in new things without getting disoriented. Collaboration: To be able to put across your ideas and solutions to people, including students, professionals, college-goers or startup founders, you need to be able to connect and communicate with people effortlessly. Polishing your networking skills, learning how to present your thoughts in meetings and working with people are the bedrock of your ability to collaborate.

Anything that helps the students to achieve new skills. We will provide a tentative yearly plan to every club so that they can focus on right things — however, this plan can be (should be) challenged by the members of the club and make it better and suitable for them. We will stick to our values when we select the topics/projects. If you are looking for self-learning, collaborative learning and contribution to your team’s learning, this is what it’ll look like: workshops, bootcamps, weekly/bi-weekly meet-ups, hosting conference - in short, from Augmented Reality workshops to how to create a LinkedIn profile — all with a pinch of fun.  We also hone talent to compete in international technical contests, hackathons and challenges. li.

Coding is more than just a career skill. It is a language of creativity and problem-solving. Coding is a fundamental skill in the digital age that we live in. At Campus Community, we show kids their unlimited creative potential and help them leverage their coding skills for problem-solving.
